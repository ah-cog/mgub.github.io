moko.im

I'm an engineer, artist, and teacher living in the San Francisco Bay Area. My work focus on the design and use of new kinds of tools for creative expression. Most of my work explores the dual roles and themes of creativity and automation (and intention?) in productive processes, creativity in automation, automation as creativity.

I started Computer Camp to advance the state of interactive system design tools that combine computational design techniques and automation. Computer Camp combine creative process, computational design, artificial intelligence, and teaching to the efficiency of creative expression.

Before that, I earned a Master's Degree in Human-Computer Interaction from the University of Maryland, College Park, a B.S. in Computer Science emphasizing Artificial Intelligence and a minor in Mathematics.
